# Leonardo da Vinci

Leonardo da Vinci was a true Renaissance polymath, excelling in a wide range of disciplines including painting, sculpture, architecture, engineering, science, and invention. Born in 1452 in Vinci, near Florence, Italy, he is renowned for creating some of the most iconic works of art in history, such as the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper. However, his talents extended far beyond the realm of art, as he made groundbreaking contributions as a scientist, mathematician, and inventor. Da Vinci's insatiable curiosity and application of the scientific method to every aspect of life, including the arts, make him a shining example of the power of interdisciplinary thinking and problem-solving.
