Hernando de Soto

Créé le 21/03/2026 • Score IA: 97/100
Hernando de Soto (c. 1497-1542) was a prominent Spanish explorer and conquistador who played a pivotal role in European expansion across the Americas during the 16th century. Born in Extremadura, Spain, to a noble family, de Soto gained prominence through his involvement in major expeditions across Central and South America. He served as a key figure in Francisco Pizarro's conquest of the Inca Empire in Peru and held the title of Governor of Cuba, appointed by King Charles V in 1536. De Soto is particularly noted for his extensive four-year expedition through southeastern North America, beginning with his landing on the Florida coast in 1539. His expeditions covered vast territories including Nicaragua, the Yucatan Peninsula, and regions along the Mississippi River, establishing him as one of the most significant explorers of the colonial era.
